Tīrtha

/ˈteer-tha/ — Sanskrit: the crossing, the ford to the far shore.

The crossing to frontier-class code, at a fraction of the cost.

A coding gateway that sends the everyday work to a fast, local model, verifies every answer, and escalates to a frontier model only when the work truly demands it. Cheaper, because most code doesn't need a frontier brain. Trustworthy, because nothing ships unchecked.

How it works

Cheap, but checked.

Tīrtha is a cascade, not a coin-flip. The work flows down to the smallest model that can be trusted with it — and trust is something we measure, never assume.

1

A fast local model drafts.

Most coding tasks are routine. A small, cheap model handles them in-house — no frontier bill, no round-trip.

2

Every answer hits the wall.

The draft is run, tested, and checked against what correctness actually requires. We don't trust the output — we verify it.

3

Only the hard part escalates.

When verification can't vouch for the draft, and only then, Tīrtha calls a frontier model to finish the job.

You pay frontier prices for frontier problems. Nothing else.

Why it holds

The honesty is the moat.

Anyone can route to a cheaper model and hope. Tīrtha's edge is the verification layer that makes "cheaper" safe — and a refusal to claim more than we can show.

Frontier-class accuracyValidated on rigorous, contamination-controlled public benchmarks — not the easy, leaked ones.
Verified, not trustedAnswers are checked by execution before they reach you. The gate is the product.
Tunable, by designDial the accuracy-to-cost trade-off to fit the job — strict for production, loose for drafts.

We publish what we measure and we say it plainly. If a claim can't survive a skeptical engineer, it doesn't go on this page.
